# TRV-SHIELD Safety Framework

## Human Safety First

TRV-SHIELD exists to protect human life, public health, ecological stability, and long-term environmental resilience.

All operational decisions, infrastructure priorities, telemetry systems, and deployment strategies must prioritize prevention of human harm before operational convenience or political optics.

Built under the DOP-AI doctrine:

> Dignity Over Power

---

# Core Safety Principles

## Prevention Before Collapse

Environmental infrastructure should prevent failure conditions before public harm occurs.

TRV-SHIELD prioritizes:

- upstream interception
- surge equalization
- predictive telemetry
- redundancy systems
- flood-state operational continuity
- fail-soft infrastructure behavior

---

## No Silent Failures

Critical infrastructure failures must never occur invisibly.

All bypasses, overflow events, treatment interruptions, telemetry outages, or emergency diversions should be:

- timestamped
- sensor-logged
- operationally reviewed
- publicly auditable
- archived for analysis

---

## Fail-Soft Infrastructure Design

Systems should degrade safely instead of collapsing catastrophically.

Fail-soft principles include:

- modular treatment cells
- distributed telemetry
- independent operational zones
- emergency routing
- gravity-priority flow systems
- manual override capability
- redundancy layers

---

## Flood-State Operational Continuity

TRV-SHIELD infrastructure should maintain critical operational capability during:

- severe rainfall
- surge flow events
- flooding
- partial power loss
- infrastructure overload
- equipment failure

Critical operational systems should remain isolated from major flood risk where possible.

---

# Operational Safety Zones

## Zone 1 — River Interface

Flood-tolerant dirty-side infrastructure.

Primary risks:
- surge flow
- debris impact
- contamination exposure
- erosion
- overflow conditions

Safety priorities:
- interception continuity
- debris management
- telemetry survivability

---

## Zone 2 — Equalization & Buffer

Surge stabilization infrastructure.

Primary risks:
- overflow events
- surge overload
- sediment accumulation
- structural stress

Safety priorities:
- surge containment
- overflow prevention
- operational buffering

---

## Zone 3 — Treatment Cells

Protected modular treatment infrastructure.

Primary risks:
- treatment interruption
- pump failure
- disinfection degradation
- throughput overload

Safety priorities:
- redundancy
- cell isolation capability
- modular failover routing

---

## Zone 4 — Critical Control

Protected operational coordination systems.

Primary risks:
- telemetry outage
- communication failure
- power interruption
- emergency coordination failure

Safety priorities:
- backup power
- telemetry continuity
- emergency escalation capability
- protected operational intelligence systems

---

# Telemetry Safety Standards

Telemetry systems should support:

- real-time environmental visibility
- operational anomaly detection
- escalation forecasting
- sensor validation
- infrastructure status monitoring
- public reporting systems

Potential monitored inputs:

- H₂S
- E. coli
- turbidity
- dissolved oxygen
- rainfall
- flow volume
- flow velocity
- treatment throughput
- bypass state
- pump status

---

# Emergency Escalation Standards

Critical events should trigger:

- operational alerts
- telemetry logging
- escalation review
- incident archiving
- public reporting procedures
- infrastructure inspection requirements

Emergency escalation should prioritize clarity, speed, and public transparency.

---

# Manual Override Doctrine

Critical infrastructure systems should maintain manual operational capability during:

- automation failure
- telemetry disruption
- communications outages
- emergency flood-state operations

Human operators must retain the ability to stabilize the system under degraded conditions.

---

# Public Transparency & Accountability

Environmental infrastructure should not function as an invisible system.

TRV-SHIELD supports:

- public operational visibility
- incident reporting
- telemetry transparency
- environmental accountability
- human-readable risk communication

TRV-SHIELD aims to support:
