# No Silent Bypass Doctrine

## Core Principle

Critical environmental infrastructure failures must never occur invisibly.

TRV-SHIELD operates under a foundational doctrine:

> No Silent Bypass

This principle requires that all significant overflow events, bypass conditions, emergency diversions, telemetry outages, or treatment interruptions remain:

- observable
- logged
- reviewable
- explainable
- auditable

Operational invisibility increases systemic risk.

Transparency increases resilience.

---

# Why This Matters

Traditional infrastructure systems often fail silently under stress conditions.

Examples include:

- overflow discharges
- telemetry loss
- pump outages
- emergency bypasses
- partial treatment failure
- contamination spread during storms
- unreported operational degradation

When failures become invisible:

- public trust erodes
- delayed response increases harm
- environmental damage escalates
- accountability weakens
- systemic instability compounds

TRV-SHIELD treats operational visibility as a safety requirement.

---

# Operational Philosophy

The goal is not to eliminate all failures.

The goal is to eliminate invisible failures.

A visible, measurable, logged failure is safer than an unobservable one.

---

# Required Visibility Standards

All critical operational events should include:

## Timestamp

Precise event timing.

---

## Event Classification

Examples:

- overflow event
- bypass activation
- telemetry outage
- surge escalation
- containment failure
- treatment interruption

---

## Estimated Magnitude

Where possible:

- volume estimates
- duration
- affected zones
- contamination severity
- telemetry confidence levels

---

## Operational State

System status during the event.

Examples:

- normal operation
- flood-state operation
- emergency containment mode
- degraded telemetry mode

---

## Resolution Tracking

Document:

- mitigation steps
- operational recovery
- follow-up inspections
- telemetry restoration
- containment outcomes
