# Contributing to TRV-SHIELD

## Dignity Over Power

TRV-SHIELD exists to build environmental resilience systems that protect people, ecosystems, and public infrastructure before collapse occurs.

Built under the DOP-AI doctrine:

> Dignity Over Power

All contributions should align with the foundational principles of:

- prevention over reaction
- transparency over opacity
- resilience over fragility
- human-readable oversight
- fail-soft architecture
- public accountability
- environmental protection

---

# Contribution Philosophy

TRV-SHIELD is not solely a software project.

It is an interdisciplinary resilience initiative involving:

- environmental systems
- infrastructure engineering
- telemetry architecture
- operational intelligence
- flood resilience
- public-health protection
- civic transparency
- ecosystem restoration

Contributors are encouraged to think systemically and prioritize long-term public benefit.

---

# Core Contribution Principles

## Human Impact First

Technology is not the mission.

Protecting human health, environmental stability, and long-term resilience is the mission.

All proposed systems, models, telemetry layers, or infrastructure concepts should prioritize public safety and environmental protection.

---

## Prevention Before Collapse

TRV-SHIELD prioritizes systems that reduce the probability and severity of catastrophic environmental failure.

Contributions should support:

- upstream interception
- surge stabilization
- predictive telemetry
- anomaly detection
- resilience engineering
- operational continuity

---

## No Silent Failures

Operational visibility is mandatory.

Contributors should avoid architectures that allow:

- invisible bypasses
- unlogged failures
- hidden overflow events
- untraceable telemetry loss
- opaque operational behavior

Transparency is a core design requirement.

---

## Human-Readable Systems

Infrastructure should remain understandable to:

- engineers
- operators
- policymakers
- environmental scientists
- public oversight bodies
- residents

Contributors are encouraged to prioritize clarity over unnecessary complexity.

---

## Fail-Soft Design

Systems should degrade safely under stress.

Contributions should favor:

- modularity
- redundancy
- graceful degradation
- manual override capability
- flood-state survivability
- distributed operational logic
